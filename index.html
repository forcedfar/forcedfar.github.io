Twitter Account Unceremoniously Enabled

Jan 5th, 2014

What has two thumbs and can now tweet? This guy! Oops! Now you guys totally know how many thumbs I have. sigh. More leaks.

As mentioned in my inaugural post, Twitter judged me not by the content of my character, but by the provider of my free web mail. Or possibly my shady IP address. Either way, measures were taken to preempt my inevitable misbehavior and my stillborn Twitter account was unable to follow or tweet or be followed or anything useful. I opened a support ticket and received no response, but upon checking today I found my Twitter account to be fully functional. I proceeded to follow a few people taking care to give away as little about myself as possible.

This is a positive outcome, for certain. Also, as a bonus, my support ticket remains open. This lends me hope that they may accidentally upgrade my privileges once more and I will be granted use of the coveted one-hundred-and-forty-first character.

The Burden of Anonymity

Dec 31st, 2013

I am one of approximately 7.13 billion people on this planet. Ideally, an adversary would have a one in 7.13 billion chance of connecting Forced Far to my real life identity. However, it is trivial to narrow this down and assume that I am a member of a much smaller subset of the world’s population. I am an English-speaking internet user. I know how to use Tor. There are currently approximately three million Tor users. According to some rough estimates and quick back-of-the-envelope math, less than a million Tor users live in English-speaking countries. Even taking every privacy precaution, my blog leaks enough information about me to eliminate over 99.986% of the population. A keen reader will observe that in making those calculations I essentially outed myself as one who lives in an English-speaking country. Leaks upon leaks.
