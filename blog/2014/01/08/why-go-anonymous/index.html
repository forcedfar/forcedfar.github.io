Why Go Anonymous

Jan 8th, 2014

In a previous post I wrote about the difficulty of maintaining my anonymity online. This provokes the question: Why is my anonymity worth the hassle? I have not committed, nor intend to commit any crimes. I do not currently have any stalkers. I am not under investigation by an adversary powerful enough to circumvent the pseudonymity already offered me by my ISP and mainstream internet services. So why am I blogging anonymously?

Actual freedom of speech

The Constitution of the United States of America grants to its citizens the right to freedom of speech. This right is rarely exercised to its full extent. Anything we say can and will be held against us and so many of us choose to amend our thoughts or say nothing at all. Behind the mask of Forced Far I can finally speak my mind and I’m quite excited to find out what my unfettered mind has to say.

To see if I can

Is it even possible today? How hard is it? What are the roadblocks? I already discovered that mainstream email accounts cannot be had anonymously and my Twitter account was suspended until just recently. We live in a window in history where it is possible to share our thoughts anonymously and I feel I would be ungrateful if I didn’t take advantage of that. I have the added benefit that my blog might serve as something of a guide to someone who needs anonymity more than I do.

To escape my own perfectionism

I am an inexperienced writer. I have many ideas that I would blog about, but I stress and worry so much about each one that my output is very, very low. The result of this is that I don’t practice writing and I don’t get any better. An anonymous blog affords me the opportunity to write freely about my passions without fear of criticism. I can make mistakes and I can change my mind later. Worse comes to worse, I can always throw away Forced Far and begin again with a different pseudonym. I would say that publishing on the internet is like writing something in stone, but stone etchings are susceptible to erosion and will eventually break down. Digital content is forever.

Protecting myself and others

I have thoughts and theories that I plan to share that are likely going to piss off powerful groups of people. I have a way of life and people I love and things to lose. I’m having trouble finishing this paragraph without being a little ashamed that I’m treating myself as some kind of tortured superhero trying to maintain his secret identity. I might not have any super powers. I might end up just a regular nobody in spandex, but I won’t really know until I put on the cape.
